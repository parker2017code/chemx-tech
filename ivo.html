Deposition Platform

Hardware specifications.

The xemX system is built around magnetron co-sputtering with up to 7 cathode sources operating simultaneously in a single run. The co-sputtering geometry produces a continuous lateral composition gradient across a 100 mm wafer, yielding 342 unique thin-film compositions per campaign.

Composition Library Geometry

Each cathode contributes a flux gradient across the wafer. The overlap of up to 7 independent gradients produces a 2D composition map across the ternary or higher-order composition space in a single run.

For quaternary systems like Al-Sc-Gd-N, the three-cation composition space can be sampled across the full ternary triangle in one wafer, rather than along a 1D gradient as used in typical sequential or single-target combinatorial approaches.

Relevance to Piezoelectric Nitride Work

XRD phase mapping is the most directly applicable tool for AlScN and AlScGdN work. Automated mapping identifies the wurtzite stability window, detects amorphous or secondary phase formation, and resolves c-axis texture across all 342 composition points in a single automated run, precisely the characterization needed to map phase stability as a function of Sc and Gd content.
Piezoelectric coefficient (d33) mapping: Whether d33 mapping is available in the xemX characterization suite should be confirmed with Lars. If not available on-wafer, d33 measurement on shortlisted candidate compositions at your facility is a natural follow-on step after phase stability mapping.
Ferroelectric switching characterization (P-E loops, coercive field) is not currently in the automated suite. Whether this can be added or arranged via external partners: confirm with Lars. The xemX campaign identifies composition-phase relationships and d33 trends; ferroelectric property validation on shortlisted candidates would be the logical next step at Akoustis.

What your papers tell us about this campaign.

Phase space is the main benefit of adding Gd. The quaternary Al-Sc-Gd-N system sustains higher total substitution (x+y) within the wurtzite phase compared to binary AlScN alone, expanding the accessible metastable phase space. Compositions that would be phase-unstable in ternary AlScN become accessible in the quaternary, with correspondingly higher d33 and lower Ec. Gd also costs significantly less than Sc, which matters at production scale.

Ferroelectric switching confirmed at low total substitution. Al0.83Sc0.15Gd0.02N shows P-E hysteresis with a coercive field of ~4.9 MV/cm, which matches the performance of Al0.7Sc0.3N despite requiring considerably less Sc. For BAW and reconfigurable RF applications, this demonstrates that Gd co-substitution delivers meaningful electrochemical work even at low fractions.

N2:Ar ratio is a primary process variable, not a secondary one. Above y = 0.15 Gd, N2-rich conditions produce wurtzite + rocksalt coexistence; Ar-rich conditions produce amorphous films. Phase-pure wurtzite is robust across both conditions only for y ≤ 0.15. The xemX campaign will document N2:Ar explicitly as part of the deposition record.

Broad mixed-phase regions at high substitution are expected. Kinetic trapping during sputtering makes the wurtzite-to-rocksalt transition gradual rather than sharp. Gradual mixed-phase regions in XRD data are therefore physically normal, not process failures. The automated XRD mapping will identify the exact phase boundary across all 342 composition points.

Oxygen contamination is the known defect risk in sputtered AlScN. O substitutionals and Al vacancies introduced during reactive sputtering act as domain wall pinning centers, degrading switching and retention. Base pressure, target conditioning, and gas purity are the practical handles. These will be documented and minimised in process setup.

Multiferroic ordering has not yet been demonstrated. Long-range magnetic order requires a Gd percolation threshold of y ≥ 0.195, yet the maximum phase-pure wurtzite achieved to date is only y = 0.15. The pathway is scientifically credible; however, reaching it will require higher effective growth temperatures than NREL achieved. A combinatorial campaign mapping the upper boundary of the wurtzite phase field is the essential first step toward multiferroic functionality.

A single xemX campaign spanning x+y = 0.10 to 0.35 under N2-rich reactive conditions would generate a complete phase stability and d33 trend map across the full Al-Sc-Gd ternary, directly identifying candidate compositions for device-level validation at your facility.

Service Model

How we work with partners.

xemX operates as a contract combinatorial materials service based at Ruhr-Universität Bochum. A project can be scoped as a single exploratory campaign or a structured multi-round program with Bayesian-guided iteration.

Project structure

A project begins with a scoping discussion to define the composition space, characterization targets, substrate requirements, and deliverable format. Multi-round programs use earlier results to direct subsequent campaigns toward the most informative sub-spaces.

Deliverables

Standard deliverables include the full composition-property dataset, automated XRD phase maps, EDX composition maps, and any additional characterization outputs agreed during scoping. Data is delivered in analysis-ready format with processed visualizations.

From campaign to prototype

Once a campaign identifies target compositions, controlled uniform depositions on substrates of your choice are available for downstream validation and device-level measurement at your facility. This bridges combinatorial screening to integration testing.

Exclusivity options

Options including data exclusivity windows, IP assignment on compositions identified during a campaign, or material system exclusivity during an engagement can be discussed on a per-project basis.

Typical project timeline

A single campaign from kick-off to data delivery runs several weeks. Multi-round programs are scoped with milestone-based timelines. Lead times depend on queue position and target preparation requirements.

Target material sourcing

xemX sources standard targets directly. You noted in your March 4 email that Japanese vendors can supply uncommon binary alloy targets for materials like Gd, which is noted as a useful parallel path.
